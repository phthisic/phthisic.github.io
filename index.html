    Introduction

    This project is a website combined history and music to give the target audiences an experience of WW1. By using the data from Queensland database, we are able to reproduce the music and link it to the historical photos. As the main focus is the experience of the life and art during the WW1period, the name of the website is “The war of theirs.”

    I am the programmer in the team and take all the responsibility of the code. However, I also acted as a designer and actively giving my idea about the website. The website is mainly based on PHP, HTML, CSS and JavaScript. HTML together with CSS forms the appearance and data is read and presented by PHP and JavaScript.

    Two database have been used:
    State Library of Queensland - Music Queensland - World War 1 State Library of Queensland - The Queenslander newspaper photographs 1914-1918


    Design Exploration

    When I first explore the qld.data, I want to find something fun and artistic. Music is just what I want. Thus, I decided to use the data of WW1 music. My initial thought is to use the music to teach history, as there are also many databases of the WW1.

    I noticed that there is music sheet in the database which can be used to teach the audience music notes. Thus, my idea became to used the music sheet to present both the historical music and knowledge about music notes.

    Based on the finding that there are plenty of WW1 photos in the database. I came up with another idea that a big picture formed by little pieces of photos can be used to present the history.

    Reflection on the methods to generate ideas:

    The main method has been used for getting design idea is gap-filling. There are many data on the website that can be used, but in the meanwhile, it was difficult to decide what and how to use them. Thus, what I did, was to write down the data that might be used and some different kind or style of websites. I'm required to think of how the data can be used to link to the websites.

    This method narrowed down the data and helped me think of more detailed ways of using it. The process is easier but the idea can also be limited.

    Another method is five whys. Actually, this method did not help a lot as I always stopped and could not continue to ask. I think this method can work better in a team rather than only by myself. What's more, I think this method can give some detailed idea of using the data so it might work better when I finally decide what data to use and try to find a way to use it.

    Redesign the idea:

    Ideas has been quickly changed through the methods. I focused on the two main idea and tried to find a way to having both the music and photos in my website. Then, considering the initial aim is to giving people an experience of WW1, instead of teaching the music notes, I decided to use the music notes to present to historical photos. Each notes is a picture and when users hover on them, the picture would shows.

    Reflection on the methods to redesign ideas:

    I used brainstorming, brainstorming and mind map during this period. I think the combination of brainstorming and mind map can help us organize the data efficiently if we can select the data firstly. This is also the best research method I used during the whole process and the design method I want to recommend to the team.

    However, some methods did not work with my design. The black hats of the "six-hats" cannot help me efficiently because its function is finding the drawback during the process. With the help of the "relevance" in the RESEARCH method, I can figure out my drawback clearly.


    PartA

    What I have done for part A is mainly contributing my opinion about the website. After the pitch at the fourth week, it’s time to become a group. A very useful method here is the world café. As my first choice of the database is music, I spent some time at the music table. What we discuss about the music is focused on the target audiences and domain. At first, we thought about the website can be designed for the old people who want to recall the old days back to WW1. But in the second round, one of the classmates pointed out that WW1 is too far away from nowadays.

    So, we reconsidered it, starting from the domain. The domain can be divided into two parts, music and history. Thus, the website can be designed for music lovers who want to know about music during the war. Or people who want to learn music sheet as there are many photos of music notes. Or people who want to collect the music covers. Also, the website can be designed for people who want to explore the Queensland’s history during the WW1 period. This part of discussion was used when the group begin to work.

    The group was formed after the world café activity and luckily, all of our group members were interested in the music element. During the discussion around part A, I contribute to the key interaction of our website, the music sheet. Allowing users interact with the music sheet, they can explore the historical photos which are in the same time when the music was produced.

    What’s more, as we noticed that there is only music sheet in the database, and we could not find the resources of music, we were facing the possibility that we had to give up this idea. Finally, I found a software called Musescore and successfully reproduce a piece of music. It was not hard since I have learned piano for several years, but it did make sure that our idea could be developed further.

    Reflection on part A

    Part A was quite unobstructed as our group members, at most of the time, are quite quickly to reach an agreement. Our idea was mainly based on the music sheet, music player and the photos. When we discussed the interact flow, we were not quite sure about whether we should build all the things in one single page, or separate them to several pages. One good thing I think we have done is that we did not just give up the other idea. On contrary, we decided to see which one is better when building the demo.


    PartB

    There is not much time for us to build the demo. Thus, we quickly discussed on the interaction flow and then begin to work on the coding.

    Since we organized the content and realized that the music and photo content should be two separate part, we picked up the other interaction flow that had not been used in the part A. What’s more, we added the music player in every page to always allow the users access to the music.

    Reflection on part B

    The coding part of the team work actually did not go very well, as I am not satisfied with the other programmer's work. The initial plan was that the other programmer would code a rough demo and I would complete the detail and finish the demo. However, when I got the rough demo, I was extremely disappointed. It was true that the demo was following our discussion, but I really could agree that he treated it seriously.

    For instance, in the sketch, we had a navigation bar at the top of each picture. Obviously, this is a primary navigation bar fixing at the top of each page, and when users browse each section in the page, the navigation bar would always be there. However, in the rough demo, there were several navigation bars in one page. He gave each section a navigation.

    The result was that I rebuilt the pages and talked about this among the group. We finally decided to let the other programmer become a designer and I would code all the rest works.

    When I review the whole issue, I realized that I was not good at the team work part as I could just tell him that the quality of the rough demo is not satisfying. For the next time when I work in a team, I would focus more on the communication with the team members.


    PartC

    From this part, as I am the only programmer in our team, I spent all my time on the coding and negotiating with the other three designers. I wanted to implement as much as we want to have. However, the fact is there were many things I need to learn to complete the website. Thus, building the final prototype took me a really long time.

    My working process has been recorded by the journal. Starting with the home page, then move to the music page, and finally I finished the photo page and the connecting between music and photo pages.

    Reflection on part C

    PHP is a whole new language for me. This is the first back-end language that I have ever used. However, now I have to say, PHP is really great.

    When I use html and want to change a link in the primary navigation, I have to change all the pages. But with PHP, I can only change one single php, and it would apply to all the pages. What’s more, the process of reading data from JS and pass to the PHP is elegant. I really enjoy using PHP. However, I still found that I was rewriting some sentences in each PHP and I did not know what should I do to put them all in one function. Because of the time limitation, I did not review my coding and made it looks better.


    Portfolio

    I enjoy building my portfolio, this is a chance for me to build a website that I always want to have. I have thought about the vertical navigation for a long time, but I did not have the change to code it as either the website is design to meet the users demand or I was in a team work.

    To finish this website, I viewed some websites with vertical navigation to find some design inspiration. At last, I focused on Origin’s software.

    The vertical navigation is always at the left side of the website. Users’ location in the website is always clear so I did not use the breadcrumb. What’s more, I tried to cancel some pages’ title. For example, in the work page, there are only the gallery of the works, but the location is obvious as the vertical navigation has been highlighted.


    Reflection on Course

    Reflection on methods

    First of all, one thing that has been went through the whole semester is the journal. In this semester, I kept journals for three courses, which actually, gave me lots of stress. I am not a person that used to keep a journal, not in before, and I do not think I am now, liking it. I can understand that the aim of keeping a journal is to document the idea changes and design process, it is also a good way to record all the works that I have done. However, it also took large amount of time for me to write them down. If the journal is just for recording, I think there are many other ways that works better than writing journal. But if it is for help generate new ideas, I really do not think it helps a lot. Maybe I have used the journal in a wrong way.

    I like to use notes to record my new ideas as it is always accessible. I can stick it on the wall where I always can see. Sometimes, when looking at the notes, I can find the connection between them and it generate new ideas. Notes is not a good a way to document things. Once I use the idea, I just drop that piece of notes. However, it does help me a lot in this course, better than the journal.

    Another issue is that, I always taken photos of the work had done in the course and planned to organize it after the course. However, the fact is that I almost never reviewed them. Taking photos is a good thing but I really need to use them in a right way. Just press the shutter would not help me memorize anything or generate anything.

    Reflection on course content and team work

    For the course content, it is interesting to use the database to build a website. The process of thinking which databases can be used, how they can be connected, what is the possible way to display them are all exciting.

    Through the team work, it is hard to say that the cooperation is perfect. In the last semester, I worked with other two team members in the DECO7110, and we were happy with each other. Thus, in this time’s team work, the new team member was obviously not fit into us. I think we worked well at first, but when the new team member shows us the working process, I was the first one that questioned the quality of the work.

    For me, I have to admit that although I like to share my idea about the project, I do not like to communicate about others work, even they need suggestions. I always just tell them " stop doing that, I will take the work from now." I think this would make others really upset.


    For Future Study

    I might try the journal again, when I do not have to keep three journals at same time. I believe this can be a useful method, and I just need to figure out the right way to use it. Instead of just thinking about the work I have done, I need more time to thinking about how well I have used the journal.

    I would also think more about how I should cooperate with other team members. I really need to trust in others, and believe they can also do the work very well. This can help me build a good relationship between others and also, reduce my work load.

    Symphony (DECO7110)

    The designed installation was named “Symphony,” which was an art installation prompting people to trigger different parts of the music and then remix together. When one user interacts with the installation, one type of music would be activated, as more and more people getting involved in the installation, the played music would be more pleasant.

    The installation of “Symphony” is planned to install by the UQ Lake in a place where people have relaxing willing. And the purpose of this installation is to add some music elements into a place, making people feel comfortable and enjoying the environment with smooth melodies and the embrace of nature. The purpose of the project is to create a collective experience and enchanting affection for the people feel the pressure. Additionally, the way of engaging more people to interact with that is to encourage people who don’t know each other to participate with our system and evoke the passion of making new friends. The technology in this project includes the pressure-sensitive sensor to detect the users’ weight and then trigger the play of music. And the central computer will certify how many people interact with the installation so play music with different complexity.

    We are ambitious that our installation would be like by the people around the campus and the visitors. We have walked through the path of the procedure of collecting ideas and insights, analysing the needs from the users, videoing and holding two exhibitions around the campus.

    Marvin's travel (DECO7140)

    Based on the science fiction “The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy” this website is designed to educate the fundamental knowledge of universe and space physics, targeting children aged from seven to fifteen. This design report includes the background of this website and the process of design.

    The target audience is children aged between 7 and 15, who have developed the ability of abstract thinking and proper to learn simple physics knowledge. Thus, it is suitable for them to start learning the universe and space physics. The selected science fiction, published in 1979, is unlikely to be read by the target audience before and the story happened in the space. Hence, this book can serve not only as an enlightening of science fiction for children but also as exciting teaching material for universe and space physics.

    By using open card sorting, which involved six participants, the noun and verbs cards were sorted separately. After the analysis of the process of this activity, the organization system has been modified based on the feedback given by the participants.

    The group discussion went through two websites including Alibaba and Amazon. Having compared their navigation system, the clearance and necessity of each navigation system and the importance of breadcrumb are highlighted. Moreover, by creating the site map, the navigation and content can be overviewed. Using Lara, one of the personas created, and storyboard to illustrate how the user navigate in this website.

    The other group discussion focused on the website of Amazon. The consistent elements and emphasis of the visual organization have been highlighted. Considering the discussion, the visual organization of this website has been modified, and the wireframes have been produced (the wireframes include those of the first visit page, explore page, guidebook page, search page and customize color page).

    The paper prototype test, which based on the wireframes, involved three participants. After the analysis of the process and feedback, some function of buttons and prompt have been modified. Besides, during the test, the tutor pointed out that technically, there will not be a database to store the username and password. Therefore, all the functions linked to the users' account need to be redesigned.

    The aesthetics test is conducting to determaine whether the color, font, image used and button used is suitable for the content and user experience. The prototype changed in this step as the feedback from participants showed that the initial prototype was not suitable.

    With the new prototype, the aesthetics test was reconducted and based on the feedback, a hi-fi prototype was conducted. The final change isbased on the feedback of hi-fi prototype.

    Hatching monsters (DECO7230)

    “A simulation game of hatching monsters which the character can getting hungry, interacting by using a physical scene.”

    This prototype is a game of simulating the hatching and players would be hungry in the game. The idea is generated by the website “Game Generator” (figure 1.1) but the animals has changed to monsters and getting bored has changed to losing the physical and mental strength.

    The game mainly contains four parts, weighing, hatching, store and player status. The weighing system is where players weigh the eggs and decided where to hatch the eggs. The hatching system is where the players place the eggs and waiting for the monster to be born. The store is where the player sells the monster and buy more eggs or foods. Player would lose physical strength when hatching the eggs so they need to eat to stay alive. They can choose to eat cheap monster meat to increase some physical strength but would lose mental strength. Or, they can choose to eat expensive normal food to get both physical and mental strength. The game ends when either the physical or the mental strength become zero.

    To interact with the game, a pressure sensor decorated as a weighing scale and a physical platform with controllers would be used. Players use the weighing scale to weigh the eggs and the weight would display on the screen. Players can move the controller on the platform, which in the game is the action of moving in the game scene or moving the little ghost assistant. The platform is also the simulation of the store where users can choose to buy products.

    The prototype is a unity scene which contains the system of buying, weighing, hatching and collection displaying. The buying has been simplified to spend money and gain energy. Images from the Internet are used to build up the scene and some coding are used to provide the basic interaction.

    The prototype also linked to the physical controller by using Arduino. Two potentiometers are used to move to different part of the scene and move the ghost. A photocell is used to feed the players. A gravity sensor FlexiForce is used to weighing the egg. All the physical controller has been put into a box which stands for the laboratory. And some text has been written on the box to indicate the players.

    The game scene can be divided into seven parts.

    The first one is buying eggs. Players are able to buy an egg from the provider and the egg is randomly provided. Once they buy the egg, the egg would be placed on the shelf from top to bottom.

    The second one is select from the shelf. A little ghost would be here to help players select the egg. Players are able to control the ghost, moving it up and down. By short press the main control button, the ghost would take the egg from the shelf or put it back. The egg’s position is decided by the ghost position. If players long press the main control button, the ghost would put the egg on the weighing scale.

    The third one is weighing scale where players can weigh the eggs. A blackboard at back would prompt players which part of the monster is carrying by the egg.

    If players hold the stick at the right position for long enough, the number of the weight would be displayed on the weighing scale.

    Once players get the number and decided which part is the monster, they can come to the fourth part-selecting a place to hatch. The incubator has point out which part should be putted at which position. The ghost would, again, help players to put the egg at right position.

    In the fifth scene, after players putting all the eggs in the incubator, they can begin to hatch the monster. When long press the main control button and begin to hatching, all the eggs would go into the tube and players are able to begin a new round of weighing. After hatching, the monster would show up at the monster box. If there are not enough eggs on the shelf, the monster can still be hatched but the success possibility would be reduced. If the hatching is failed, players can only get ash at the end.

    However, because of the time limitation, is the evaluation, I set the success rate to 100% no matter no many eggs have been used to hatch.

    The sixth part is the display part, where players can choose to display their monsters after hatching. However, the function hasn’t been fully built as it is not crucial in this prototype to be tested.

    Players are expected to first put the eggs on the weighing scale and then press the button to weigh the egg. After knowing the weight, they need to check which position is the best place to put the egg and when the four position all have their eggs, players can begin to hatching.

    The last part is the player status data. The data includes two parts, money and energy. Every time players buy an egg, it would cost money, and hatching monsters can give the money back. If a monster is been successfully hatched, players can get more money than the eggs cost. However, there is a chance that players could not success, so that is when they can only get a little money.

    The action of weighing and hatching can cost energy. When the energy become 0, the game ends. But the money can be spent to buy energy. This is regarded as the simulation of selling monsters and buying food.
